# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Anti-Malicious Use Policy

### Strictly Prohibited:

❌ **Malware Development**
- Do NOT use this code to create viruses, trojans, or ransomware
- Do NOT distribute malicious versions
- Do NOT weaponize security features

❌ **Unauthorized Access**
- Do NOT use for hacking or penetration testing without authorization
- Do NOT bypass security measures for illegal purposes
- Do NOT facilitate data theft or privacy violations

❌ **Harmful Modifications**
- Do NOT add backdoors or vulnerabilities intentionally
- Do NOT create phishing variants
- Do NOT distribute compromised versions

❌ **Misrepresentation**
- Do NOT claim original authorship
- Do NOT remove attribution or copyright notices
- Do NOT rebrand as your own without proper attribution

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Examples of Positive Behavior:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- **Using the software ethically and legally**
- **Contributing security improvements**
- **Reporting vulnerabilities responsibly**

### ❌ Examples of Unacceptable Behavior:

- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate
- **Creating or distributing malicious versions**
- **Facilitating illegal activities**
- **Intentionally introducing vulnerabilities**
- **Removing security features**

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

Examples of representing our community include:
- Using an official e-mail address
- Posting via an official social media account
- Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event
- **Distributing modified versions of this software**
- **Creating derivative works**
- **Commercial implementations**

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at **security@nebula3ddev.com**.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### Enforcement Actions:

1. **Warning** - First offense, minor violation
2. **Temporary Ban** - Repeated violations, moderate severity
3. **Permanent Ban** - Severe violations, malicious intent
4. **Legal Action** - Criminal activity, malware distribution

---

## Malicious Use Detection

We actively monitor for:
- Malicious forks and derivatives
- Backdoor insertions
- Vulnerability exploitation
- Unauthorized distributions
- Copyright violations

**If detected, we will:**
1. Issue DMCA takedown notices
2. Report to GitHub/hosting platforms
3. Notify security authorities if applicable
4. Pursue legal action if necessary
